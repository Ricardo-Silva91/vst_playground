Drum Processor
Everything a drum hit needs to become itself.

Drum Smash is a complete drum character engine in a single plugin. 21 parameters across 8 processing sections take any drum source from clean and clinical to destroyed, saturated, smashed, and spatial. Ships with 8 factory presets that cover the full range of lo-fi to loud.

macOS Windows
Signal Chain
Pitch / Wow› Bit Crusher› Saturation› Noise / Crackle› Transient› HPF› LPF› Compressor› Reverb› Stereo Width› Output Gain

The chain is ordered destructively first (pitch, crush, saturate, noise) then restorative (filter, compress, spatial). This means character is printed before any cleanup happens. Noise and crackle are gated by input signal level and won't fire during silence.

Processing Sections
Crusher
Bit Depth / Sample Rate Reduction

Reduces bit depth and sample rate to introduce digital grit and aliasing. Even subtle crushing adds texture that reads as "character" without obvious lo-fi. Extreme values produce the classic 12-bit drum machine crunch.

Saturation
Drive / Saturation Type

Adds harmonic distortion. Different saturation types produce different flavors: soft-clip roundness, tape-style even harmonics, or harder asymmetric clipping. Drive controls how far into the nonlinear region the signal is pushed.

Character
Noise Amount / Crackle

Injects broadband noise and random crackle gated to the drum signal. Simulates dust, record surface noise, and tape hiss that only appear when something is actually playing. Noise adds air; Crackle adds vinyl intimacy.

Filters
HPF Freq / LPF Freq

High-pass and low-pass filters placed after the destructive processing. HPF removes mud built up from saturation and low-frequency noise. LPF rolls off harshness from bit crushing. Use both to carve the character into shape.

Compressor
Threshold / Ratio / Attack / Release

Post-distortion compression glues the processed signal together. Fast attack kills the transient for a smashed pumping sound. Slow attack lets the attack through then compresses the body. The compressor acts on already-destroyed audio, compounding the effect.

Reverb
Reverb Size / Reverb Mix

Room reverb placed at the end of the destructive chain. Because it reverberates the already-processed signal, the tail carries all the noise, grit, and saturation of the processed hit rather than the clean original. The room sounds damaged.

Modulation
Pitch Shift / Wow Rate / Wow Depth

Pitch shift transposes the entire hit. Wow applies the same LFO modulation from Pitch Wobble to the drum signal. Pitch shift downward adds weight; combined with wow it produces the unstable tape sound of a machine running slightly slow.

Spatial
Stereo Width / Output Gain / Dry-Wet

Stereo Width widens the processed signal on the mid-side axis. Output Gain compensates for level changes introduced by saturation and compression. Dry-Wet blends the full processed chain against the original, so you can use the plugin as a parallel processor.

Factory Presets
Dusty Vinyl

Gentle bit reduction, moderate crackle and noise, light compression. Sounds like a drum break sampled off a worn 45.

Heavy Hitter

High saturation drive, hard compression with slow attack. The hit punches through with maximum impact and a driven mid-range.

Bit Crusher

Aggressive bit depth and sample rate reduction. Aliasing artifacts become part of the texture. Very low bit depth produces stepped, almost melodic distortion on the decay.

Lo-Fi Radio

Heavy LPF, moderate noise, light reverb. Sounds like a drum machine heard through a cheap clock radio speaker.

Punchy Club

Fast compressor with high ratio, wide stereo image, surgical HPF. Maximum impact and modern club-ready density.

Tape Warped

Pitch shift down combined with wow modulation and tape saturation. The entire hit sounds like it's playing off a machine with failing capstan.

Nuclear Snare

Maximum saturation, fast compression, boosted reverb. The snare explodes. Everything after the transient is compressed into a loud, roomy smash.

Bedroom Boom-Bap

The full lo-fi package: bit reduction, vinyl noise and crackle, warm LPF, slight pitch down. Sits perfectly under dusty piano and bass samples.

Use Cases
Parallel Drum Bus Processing

Set Dry-Wet to 50-70% and route your full drum bus through Drum Smash. The plugin adds character and glue without replacing the original signal. Saturation on the bus compresses peaks psychoacoustically, making the kit feel more cohesive and powerful in the mix.

Preset: Heavy Hitter as starting point, back off Dry-Wet to taste
Resample and Destroy a Loop

Feed a clean, modern drum loop through Drum Smash fully wet. Print the output as a new audio file. The processed version becomes your lo-fi source, ready to layer under the original or use standalone. Bit Crusher and Tape Warped presets work especially well for this.

Dry-Wet: 1.0, then print/export the result
Single Hit Sculpting

Use on an individual kick, snare, or hi-hat sample in a sampler. Each section lets you shape the exact character of the hit: HPF to remove flub, saturation to add body, compressor to control transient shape, reverb to place it in a space. More precise than applying a preset to a whole bus.

Start with filters and compressor before engaging destruction
Tips and Workflow
▶

The chain order is your friend. Because destruction happens before cleanup, you can push saturation and bit crush hard and then use the HPF and LPF to carve out the unusable frequencies. The filters don't undo the distortion — they shape it.

▶

Noise and crackle are gated. They only appear when the drum signal is above threshold, so they won't add noise to silence between hits. This means you can push them fairly high without ruining the track between hits.

▶

Slow compressor attack for punch, fast for smash. A slow attack (30-80ms) preserves the transient click and then ducks the body, giving snappy punch. A fast attack (1-5ms) clamps the whole hit, creating a flat, heavily processed smash. Both are useful; they produce completely different results.

▶

Pitch shift down for weight. Even -1 or -2 semitones makes a kick drum sound significantly bigger and heavier. Combined with saturation, the increased fundamental frequency pushes more energy into the low midrange where most speakers actually reproduce bass.

▶

Use the Dry-Wet for parallel compression. At 50% wet, the plugin functions as a parallel compressor and saturator simultaneously. The clean transients of the dry signal punch through while the processed wet signal provides density and character underneath.

▶

Reverb on destroyed audio is its own thing. Because the reverb is post-destruction in the chain, the tail of the hit decays through all the noise and grit. This gives the reverb an intimate, room-mic quality rather than a pristine space. Keep the reverb mix low for a subtle sense of space, or push it for a full damaged-room effect.
