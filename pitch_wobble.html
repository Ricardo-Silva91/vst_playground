The instability that makes it feel alive.

Pitch Wobble applies a sinusoidal LFO to the pitch of the incoming signal. A smoothing stage makes the modulation curve feel organic rather than mechanical. The result ranges from subtle tape-style drift to lurching seasick detuning, depending on how far you push Depth and Rate.

macOS Windows
Signal Chain
Input › LFO Generator › Smoother › Pitch Shift › Output

A sinusoidal LFO oscillates at the frequency set by Rate, with amplitude determined by Depth in cents. Before it reaches the pitch shifter, the modulation signal passes through a low-pass smoother whose cutoff is controlled by Smoothness. High smoothness rounds off the sinusoid, making the pitch drift feel lazy and organic. Low smoothness lets the full waveshape through, producing tighter, more mechanical vibrato.

Parameters
Depth (cents)

Sets the maximum pitch deviation in cents. 1-5 cents is almost subliminal, adding life to an otherwise static source. 10-30 cents produces noticeable tape-style warble. Above 50 cents the effect becomes obviously detuned and dramatic.

Range: 0 → 100+ cents
Rate (Hz)

Controls how fast the pitch oscillates. Below 1 Hz creates slow, drifting movement. 2-6 Hz is the classic vibrato range. Above 8 Hz starts to feel nervous and unstable. Very high rates produce a tremolo-like roughness at the pitch level.

Range: 0.1 → 20+ Hz
Smoothness

Filters the LFO waveform before it hits the pitch shifter. Low values preserve the sharp edges of the sine, giving a crisper, more traditional vibrato. High values blur and delay the modulation, producing the irregular, weighted feel of a stretched tape spool slowing down.

Range: 0 (crisp) → 1 (sluggish)
Use Cases
Lo-Fi and Tape Character

At low depth and a slow rate with high smoothness, Pitch Wobble adds the imperceptible drift of a tape machine that's slightly off-speed. Apply to whole stems or buses for a cohesive lo-fi glue without any obvious pitch movement.

Depth: 3-8 cents / Rate: 0.3-0.8 Hz / Smoothness: 0.7-0.9
Vintage Synth Lead

Synthesizers of the 70s and 80s had oscillator instability as a feature. A moderate depth at around 3-4 Hz with medium smoothness replicates that warm, wobbly character that makes digital synths feel analog. Works especially well on saw and square wave leads.

Depth: 12-20 cents / Rate: 3-4 Hz / Smoothness: 0.4-0.6
Horror and Tension Sound Design

With high depth and a very slow rate (0.1-0.3 Hz), sustained notes develop a nauseating, unsettling quality. Stack on drones, pads, or processed voice. High smoothness makes it feel like the pitch is being dragged reluctantly rather than oscillating cleanly.

Depth: 40-80 cents / Rate: 0.1-0.3 Hz / Smoothness: 0.8-1.0
Detuned Choir / Ensemble Effect

Run several instances on duplicate vocal or string tracks, each with slightly different Rate and phase start positions. Each voice wobbles independently, creating the natural detuning of a real ensemble where no two players are in perfect unison.

Depth: 5-15 cents / Rate: 2-5 Hz (vary per instance) / Smoothness: 0.5
Tips and Workflow
▶

Small depth, always. The most musical uses of this plugin are at depths you can barely hear consciously. 3-10 cents on a vocal or pad adds life without ever reading as "pitch effect." Resist going bigger unless drama is the goal.

▶

Rate below the note duration. If your notes are quarter notes at 120 BPM (500ms each), a rate of 2 Hz means the pitch completes a full cycle in 500ms. Matching or sub-matching the rate to note length keeps the wobble from clashing with melody.

▶

Use on a send, not an insert. Running the plugin at full wet on a parallel send lets you blend in just the wobbling signal, keeping the attack and transients of the dry signal intact while the sustain drifts.

▶

Automate Depth for expression. Start at 0 cents and automate the depth upward during a sustain or hold. The note begins stable and gradually drifts, mimicking the natural swell of vibrato a vocalist adds as a note progresses.

▶

Pair with a chorus for width. Pitch Wobble on center plus a chorus spread to sides gives you a lush, drifting stereo field where the center stays warm and unstable while the sides shimmer separately.
