The instability that makes it feel alive.

Pitch Wobble continuously drifts pitch by tiny random amounts, never straying far from the original — the base pitch always returns. Each movement picks a new random target and glides toward it at its own pace. The result sits between the natural micro-tuning of a live performance and the mechanical flutter of tape degradation. Not a vibrato — the movement is never regular.

macOS Windows
Signal Chain
Input› Circular Buffer› Variable-speed Read Head› Output

Pitch shifting works by reading back through a circular buffer at a slightly varying speed — faster than 1× raises pitch, slower lowers it. A new random pitch target is picked at the frequency set by Rate. The read head glides toward each target at a speed controlled by Smoothness. Because the speed variation averages out over time, the base pitch is preserved — the source never drifts permanently out of tune.

Parameters
Depth

Maximum pitch deviation in cents. At low values the effect is nearly subliminal, adding life without any obvious pitch movement. Above 20 cents it becomes clearly audible as tape-style warble. Push higher for seasick or psychedelic textures.

Range: 0 → 30 ct    Default: 8 ct
Rate

How often a new pitch target is chosen. Slow rates feel like long organic swells; fast rates feel erratic and chaotic. Very low rates produce the feeling of a tape machine warming up or slowing down.

Range: 0.1 → 5 Hz    Default: 1.0 Hz
Smoothness

How gradually the pitch glides between targets. Low values produce snappy, jerky movement. High values produce slow, continuous drift — the weighted feel of a stretched tape spool slowing down.

Range: 0.00 (snappy) → 1.00 (sluggish)    Default: 0.70

Use Cases
Vocals and voice humanisation

Adds the subtle pitch drift of a real singer. At low depth the effect is completely imperceptible as a pitch effect — it just makes the vocal feel less locked to the grid, more present and alive.

Depth: 3–8 ct / Rate: 0.5–1.5 Hz / Smoothness: 0.6–0.8
Lo-fi tape and cassette aesthetics

At low depth and slow rate with high smoothness, the effect adds the imperceptible drift of a tape machine running slightly off-speed. Apply to whole stems or buses for cohesive lo-fi glue without any obvious pitch movement.

Depth: 3–8 ct / Rate: 0.3–0.8 Hz / Smoothness: 0.7–0.9
Synth pads and drones

Breaks up the sterile perfection of digital oscillators. Run several instances on duplicate pad layers, each with slightly different Rate — each voice wobbles independently, creating the natural detuning of a real ensemble.

Depth: 5–15 ct / Rate: 2–5 Hz (vary per instance) / Smoothness: 0.5
Horror and tension sound design

With high depth and very slow rate, sustained notes develop a nauseating, unsettling quality. High smoothness makes the pitch feel like it's being dragged reluctantly rather than oscillating cleanly. Stack on drones or processed voice.

Depth: 20–30 ct / Rate: 0.1–0.3 Hz / Smoothness: 0.8–1.0
Tips and Workflow
▶

Small depth, always. The most musical uses of this plugin are at depths you can barely hear consciously. 3–10 cents on a vocal or pad adds life without ever reading as a "pitch effect." Resist going bigger unless drama is the goal.

▶

All three parameters are fully automatable. Automating Depth over a track is a particularly effective way to introduce wobble only at specific moments — fading it in on a long sustain or final note, for example.

▶

Use on a send, not an insert. Running fully wet on a parallel send lets you blend in just the wobbling signal, keeping the attack and transients of the dry signal intact while the sustain drifts.

▶

Automate Depth for expression. Start at 0 cents and automate the depth upward during a sustain. The note begins stable and gradually drifts, mimicking the natural swell of vibrato a vocalist adds as a note progresses.

▶

Pair with a chorus for width. Pitch Wobble on center plus a chorus spread to sides gives you a lush, drifting stereo field where the center stays warm and unstable while the sides shimmer separately.
