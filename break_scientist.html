Groove Processor
Make it breathe.

Break Scientist detects transients in your drum break and displaces them in time — pushing hits earlier or later than where they originally landed, then reshaping their velocities. The break starts to feel like it was programmed by a human, not quantised to a grid. Drop it on a drum break channel, pick a preset, consolidate the track — done.

macOS Windows
How It Works
Input› Transient Detection› Time Displacement› Velocity Reshape› Wet Mix› Output

The plugin uses a lookahead buffer (up to 500ms depending on settings) to detect drum transients before they arrive at the output. Each detected hit is then displaced in time according to the Swing, Humanize, and Drag values, and its amplitude is reshaped by Vel Variance. The lookahead is reported to the host as latency — the host compensates automatically during playback, so timing stays in sync while you audition. Consolidate or freeze the track before mixing — the consolidated audio has no latency offset and is the real output. Live monitoring is fine for dialling in the feel.

Timing Section
Swing

Pushes every other 16th note later. 50% is straight, 75% is a heavy shuffle, 85% is broken and extreme — the odd notes land so late the rhythm starts to feel inside-out.

50% = straight → 85% = extreme shuffle
Humanize

Adds a random micro-offset to each hit, up to ±80ms. Low values add subtle pocket feel; high values make the break sound like it's barely holding together.

Range: 0 → ±80ms per hit
Drag

Pulls every hit behind the beat, from subtle (a few ms) to extreme (up to 200ms). This is the Dilla thing — a consistent lateness that makes the groove feel heavy. At high values the break starts to feel like a different tempo entirely.

Range: 0 → 200ms behind the beat
Character Section
Sensitivity

Controls how aggressively the plugin detects drum hits. Lower for dense, washy breaks; higher for sparse, punchy ones. If hits are being missed or double-triggered, adjust this first.

Low = permissive → High = strict
Vel Variance

Randomly reshapes the volume of each hit after it's been displaced. Creates ghost hits and accents without any compression or editing — the dynamics variation emerges from the plugin, not from the source material.

Range: 0 (no change) → 1 (full randomisation)
Wet Mix

Blends the processed signal with the original. Below 100% creates a flamming effect as timed and untimed versions overlap — this can be a sound in itself on certain breaks.

Range: 0% (dry) → 100% (fully processed)
Presets
Donuts

Heavy Dilla pocket. High drag, high humanize, strong swing. Hits sit noticeably behind the beat — nothing lands where you expect, everything grooves harder for it.

MPC Straight

Classic boom bap. Clean swing, minimal humanize, almost no drag. The break swings but stays disciplined. Good starting point for straightforward hip-hop.

Drunk Shuffle

Maximum looseness. High humanize, moderate swing, light drag. Works well on slower tempos where the timing variation has room to breathe.

Jungle Step

Tight and aggressive. Light swing, very low humanize, almost no drag. Closer to Amen territory than boom bap — fast and locked-in.

Ghost Town

Velocity chaos. High vel variance, subtle timing shift. Quiet ghost hits appear between loud accents — the groove lives in the dynamics more than the timing.

Straight Up

Near-bypass. Minimal everything — just a whisper of humanize to take the sterile edge off a perfectly quantised break. Use when you want the plugin in the chain but don't want to hear it working.

Off The Grid

Maximum chaos. Extreme swing, full humanize, near-maximum drag. The break barely resembles its original rhythm. Best on slower tempos where offsets have room to breathe.

Wrong Tempo

Maximum drag. Every hit pulled back as far as possible — the break feels like it belongs to a slower song. Especially effective on fast breaks at 140+ BPM.

Slipping Away

Humanize takes over. High randomness dominates — each hit lands somewhere different every loop. No two passes sound the same. Good for chopped loops wanting variation without editing.

Trap Drunk

Heavy swing for modern rhythms. High swing (0.82) with solid drag — sits between classic Dilla feel and modern trap grid. Odd 16ths land very late; even ones stay mostly on time.

Use Cases
Sampled drum breaks

Drop it directly on a break channel. The plugin detects the transients and displaces them in real time — no manual chopping, no re-editing. Pick a preset, audition, consolidate. The Amen break becomes boom bap; the Think break starts to breathe.

Recommended preset: Donuts or MPC Straight as a starting point
Adding pocket to programmed drums

Even a perfectly programmed beat can feel mechanical. At low Humanize and modest Drag, Break Scientist adds the subliminal imprecision of a human drummer without any obviously audible timing shift. The beat still sits in the pocket — it just feels like it was played, not clicked in.

Recommended preset: Straight Up or MPC Straight / Humanize: 0.1–0.2
Layering processed and dry

Use Wet Mix below 100% to blend the displaced break with the original. The two versions phase against each other, creating a flamming effect that can range from subtle thickening to deliberate rhythmic widening. Automate the Wet Mix to bring the effect in and out.

Wet Mix: 40–70% / keep Swing and Drag moderate to avoid harsh flamming
Half-time feel from fast breaks

Crank Drag to its maximum on a break running at 140–160 BPM. The consistent behind-the-beat displacement makes the break feel like it belongs at half the tempo without changing the actual speed. The Wrong Tempo preset is built exactly for this.

Recommended preset: Wrong Tempo / works best at 130+ BPM
Tips and Workflow
▶

Always consolidate before mixing. The lookahead buffer is reported as latency to the host, which compensates automatically during playback. But the live output should not be used as a final mix source — consolidate or freeze the track first. The consolidated audio is the real output with no offset.

▶

Adjust Sensitivity for your break. Dense, washy breaks (Apache, Funky Drummer) need lower Sensitivity so the transient detector doesn't fire on every smear. Sparse, punchy breaks (Amen, Think) can take higher Sensitivity for tight per-hit displacement.

▶

Drag is the most powerful knob. Even small Drag values — 20–40ms — dramatically change how a break feels without sounding obviously processed. Start here before touching Swing or Humanize.

▶

Humanize changes every loop. Unlike Swing and Drag, which are deterministic, Humanize adds a random offset each time the break plays through. If you want the groove to be consistent loop to loop, keep Humanize at zero and use only Swing and Drag.

▶

Ghost Town preset under a tight kick. Layer a copy of your drum break running through Ghost Town at low volume under your primary kick and snare track. The ghost hits and velocity variation fill in the rhythmic space without competing with the main elements.

▶

FL Studio: right-click the parameter name label (not the slider) in the generic editor to create an automation clip. Automating Wet Mix or Drag over a section is a quick way to bring the groove effect in and out without committing to a single setting for the whole track.
